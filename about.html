A little bit about me:

I made the career transition to software engineering from my time as a building inspector and building plans examiner. During my previous career, I found that I love tackling complex projects and breaking them down into bite-sized pieces, allowing me to understand the big picture. This passion led me to software engineering, as this is a fundamental aspect of the profession. In order to build a strong technical background and successfully apply my problem solving skills, I have done extensive self-directed studying and enrolled into Rithm School to ensure my development follows the correct path.

My previous career in the building industry was rewarding and cultivated skills that are applicable to any industry. I have worked both as a consultant and for local jurisdictions as a plans examiner for three years and as a building inspector for about six months. This provided me with a diverse working environment where I had to adjust to differing stakeholders, company priorities, and working styles. Through working in diverse environments, I found that I enjoyed evaluating my situation and adjusting my approach to effectively find a solution that meets the needs of the stakeholder, such as a local jurisdiction or a homeowner.
